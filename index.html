About

The Tokajian Laboratory at the Lebanese American University, Department of Biological Sciences, investigates the genomic epidemiology of bacterial pathogens responsible for healthcare-associated and community-acquired infections in Lebanon and the broader Middle East. Our research combines whole-genome sequencing, comparative genomics, and molecular epidemiology to understand how antimicrobial resistance (AMR) emerges, spreads, and persists in clinical and environmental reservoirs.

We work primarily with Klebsiella pneumoniae, Acinetobacter baumannii, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Mycobacterium tuberculosis, and the Enterobacter cloacae complex. A central focus is the dissemination of carbapenem-resistant and extensively drug-resistant (XDR) lineages in Lebanese hospitals, including the characterization of plasmid-borne resistance determinants and mobile genetic elements that drive horizontal gene transfer across species and settings.

A major ongoing project traces the phylogeography of ST383 K. pneumoniae, a high-risk clone associated with blaKPC and blaNDM carriage, across Lebanese hospital networks. We apply Bayesian phylogenetic methods and recombination analysis to reconstruct transmission chains and estimate evolutionary rates in real time.

Metagenomic surveillance of clinical and wastewater samples allows us to profile resistome dynamics at the population level, capturing resistance gene diversity beyond what culture-based methods can detect. These datasets feed into One Health frameworks that connect clinical AMR patterns to environmental and agricultural reservoirs.
